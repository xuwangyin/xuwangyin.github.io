I build models that understand images the way humans do—not just classifying, but imagining.

One model should be enough.

Generative AI and discriminative AI have traditionally been two separate worlds—different models, different training, different applications. A classifier classifies. A generator generates. But understanding and creation are two sides of the same coin. I'm working on models that do both—robustly classify and generate high-quality images—trained with a single objective.

→ Dual Adversarial Training (arXiv)

Simplicity scales.

Energy-based models have elegant theory but notoriously unstable training. By rethinking adversarial training as a generative objective, I achieved the first stable EBM training at ImageNet 256×256 scale—matching autoregressive models, surpassing diffusion.

Safety is not an afterthought.

Previously at the Center for AI Safety, I worked on evaluating and red-teaming language models, understanding how they fail before they're deployed.
